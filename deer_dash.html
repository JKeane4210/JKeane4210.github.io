Deer Dash

Summer 2020

Role: Game Developer & 3D Artist

Type: Personal Project

Team: Kacper Mocarski (University of Illinois Chicago CS)

Description:

During the summer of 2020, my friend Kacper Mocarski wanted to work on a new project and learn something new while the world was in lockdown in the beginning months of the pandemic. This process began with several zoom calls trying to come up with ideas. Eventually, we decided on making a 3D game in the game engine Unity where the player would be driving down a highway where there are a lot of deer (or other animals) crossing the road. You would have to manage your fuel and your car's health while picking up power-ups to see how long you could last.

We also learned how to design 3D models for cars and animals in the 3D modeling software Blender. I made several animals, landscapes, and cars in the final game. This experience was valuable because it showed the amount of work it takes to make a video game. After all, in between sprites, graphics, music, sound effects, and functionality, there is so much that goes into making a great game.

Deer Dash game scene.

Project Analysis:

What Went Well:

  • "Learning How to Learn"

    I did well at seeking out good sources to learn about topics regarding game development. I had to learn C# for programming, Unity for using a game engine, and Blender for creating 3D models for the game, forcing me to find what helps me learn best.

  • Coordinating with Team

    I did well at coordinating working with my friend to assure that we each had features we could keep working on. We kept our tasks organized on Trello, and we also learned how to manage sharing/merging code using GitHub.

  • Learning the Big Picture

    I enjoyed managing every aspect of the game, ranging from audio to collisions. It made me realize the complexity of creating a video game, giving me a new respect for game development.

What Could Be Improved:

  • Maintaining Motivation

    Because we did not publish this project by the end of the summer, we have a 90% finished project sitting on the shelf. We haven't been able to polish it off because of how busy we are with school now. Unlike my LinkedIn project, there is no real driving force keeping us focused on finishing. In the future, I feel like having a stricter release date could have gotten us to a finished product.

Skills Used/Learned:

What I Learned:
  • Unity

    I learned how to use the game engine Unity this summer. Unity is a powerful engine capable of housing the production of industry-standard games.

  • C#

    Inherently from learning Unity, I learned how to program in C#, which was a new language for me. I realized that C# has a lot of similarities to Java, which I knew at the time, so I was able to apply my understanding of Java to speed up my learning.

  • Blender

    I learned how to create 3D models of animals, cars, and terrains in Blender. Through practice, I began to understand the tools for creating these models and exporting them into our game.
