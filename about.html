Design Rationale

Law of Similarity

I wanted to follow the law of similarity with my site, meaning that I would want navigation/structuring to be learned based on the look of similar aspects. This was the reason for the choices I made in creating my navigation bar. The main navigation bar is fairly standard, showing the main pages that can be entered. My main decision in my design is shown in how I showed navigation on sub-pages. My hope was that with pages like “Projects” and “Work Experience” that had subpages, I wanted to show navigation to the subpages easily in the navigation bar. Following the law of similarity, I created a sub-navigation bar that showed that subpages when you entered the “Projects” or “Work Experience” pages, making next steps intuitive.

Further, the “Projects” and “Work Experience” page both had descriptive blocks of the subpages, allowing navigation for new users, but keeping a very similar structuring for each block. Finally, each one of these subpages also had the same structuring of having a summary section, followed by an analysis section.

Aesthetic-Usability Effect

By creating a pleasing aesthetic, a site can inherently seem more usable. I tried to create a simplistic, dignified design that had a lot of very clean crisp images. I chose deep blues that create a smooth flow between the elements. Additionally, I was looking to mimic GitHub, which I already respect as a professional site. For example, my navigation bar has a similar look to GitHub’s where there is a bar with a different color from the rest of the site that underlines the current page that I am on.
